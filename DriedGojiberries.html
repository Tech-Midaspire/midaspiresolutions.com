Grades and Varieties Available
  1. Conventional Gojiberries – These are gojiberries that have been grown using conventional farming methods. They are available in a range of grades and are generally less expensive than organic goji berries.
  2. Organic Gojiberries– These are gojiberries that have been grown using certified organic farming methods. They are free from synthetic pesticides and fertilizers and are often considered to be of higher quality than conventional gojiberries.
  3. Premium Gojiberries – These are gojiberries that have been hand-selected for their large size, vibrant color, and rich flavor. They are considered to be of the highest quality and are often more expensive than other grades.
  4. Broken Gojiberries –These are gojiberries that have been damaged during the drying process and may be broken into smaller pieces. They are often less expensive than other grades and are still suitable for use in cooking and baking.

In addition to these grades, there are also several varieties of gojiberries, including the Tibetan gojiberry, the Chinese gojiberry, and the American gojiberry. These varieties can vary in terms of size, flavor, and nutritional content.


It’s worth noting that grading standards and varieties may vary depending on the producer. Regardless of the grade or variety, dried gojiberries are a nutritious and versatile ingredient that can be enjoyed in a variety of dishes or simply as a healthy snack.
